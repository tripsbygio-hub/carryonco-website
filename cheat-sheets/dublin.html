Dublin
Ireland

The craic is real. Dublin's pub culture, literary heritage, and warmth of its people make it one of the most welcoming cities in Europe. Yes, it's expensive — but done right, it's worth every cent.

☁ Best: May–Sep 💶 Currency: Euro (€) 🌡 Summer: 16–20°C ✈ Airport: DUB
🔥 Trending on TikTok & Instagram Verified picks — not just hype
01
Bunsen
Minimalist burger bar with a short, focused menu. Just burgers done perfectly. No frills, no fuss, a genuine cult following. The Nassau Street location is the original.
▶ TikTok #BunsenDublin
02
The Woollen Mills
Converted historic building right on Ha'penny Bridge. One of Dublin's best brunch spots — lovely space, local food, great service. Queue on weekends but worth it.
▶ TikTok #WoollenMills
03
Cobblestone
Authentic traditional music pub in Smithfield. Not touristy — real session music, real locals, proper pints. The antithesis of Temple Bar. Show up after 9pm on weekends.
▶ TikTok #CobblestoneSession
04
Glovers Alley
Andy McFadden's Michelin-starred restaurant in the Fitzwilliam Hotel. Creative Irish fine dining at its best — seasonal, local, and genuinely exciting. The city's top tasting menu.
▶ TikTok #GloversAlley

🍀 What to Eat + Drink
Full Irish Breakfast
Sausages, rashers (bacon), black pudding, white pudding, eggs, grilled tomato, and soda bread. Non-negotiable.
Irish Stew
Lamb or mutton with potatoes, carrots, and onions. Slow-cooked comfort food. Find it in a proper old pub.
Boxty
Traditional Irish potato pancake — half grated raw potato, half mashed. Served with smoked salmon or beef.
Colcannon
Mashed potato with kale or cabbage and lashings of butter. Soul food at its most Irish.
Guinness Stout
A good pint of Guinness takes 119.5 seconds to pour. Taste the difference here versus abroad.
Irish Whiskey
Jameson, Bushmills, Redbreast, Teeling. Ireland produces world-class whiskey — try flights at The Teeling Distillery.
Smoked Salmon
Wild Irish smoked salmon on brown soda bread with cream cheese. One of Ireland's finest simple pleasures.
Soda Bread
Dense, slightly tangy Irish bread leavened with baking soda. Order it toasted with butter at every breakfast.
☘️ Local Tips
◆ Dublin is expensive. Budget ~€60–80/day for food and activities. Book museums and attractions online to save on entry prices.
◆ Book Kilmainham Gaol and Book of Kells weeks in advance. Both are consistently sold out and cannot be easily walked into.
◆ Temple Bar is fine for one evening but massively overpriced. Drinks in nearby pubs on Dame Street cost half as much.
◆ Cobblestone in Smithfield is the real deal for traditional music. Show up after 9pm on Friday or Saturday. Free, no tourist trap.
◆ The DART coastal train is one of Europe's best urban rail journeys. Get a Leap Card for cheap travel. Howth is a must.
◆ Pack a waterproof layer — always. Irish weather changes every 30 minutes regardless of season. This is non-negotiable advice.
◆ "The craic" means good fun and lively atmosphere. Locals will tell you the craic was mighty if a pub night went well.
◆ Guinness genuinely tastes better in Dublin. The freshness and pour technique differ significantly from exports. Savour it.
🏨 Where to Stay
The Merrion ★★★★★
Georgian Dublin, D2
Four restored Georgian townhouses with an exceptional art collection. Ireland's finest hotel. Steps from Glovers Alley, National Gallery, and Grafton Street.
The Shelbourne ★★★★★
St Stephen's Green
Dublin institution since 1824. The Irish Constitution was drafted here. Lord Mayor's Lounge is legendary for afternoon tea. Classic Dublin grandeur.
The Dean Dublin ★★★★
Harcourt Street / D2
Hip, design-forward boutique hotel with rooftop bar. Popular with younger travelers. Excellent central location, great value for Dublin's luxury tier.
Budget Tip
Rathmines or Portobello
Stay south of the Liffey in Rathmines or Portobello for good apartments and B&Bs at lower prices. 15-min bus to city center.
🗺 Getting Around
Airport → Center
Aircoach or 747 bus to city center (~€8, 30–45 min). Taxi ~€30–40.
Leap Card
Contactless travel card for bus, DART, Luas tram. Saves 30% vs cash fares. Get at airport.
Walking
City center is very walkable. Trinity → St Patrick's → Guinness Storehouse all on foot.
DART Train
Coastal rail north to Howth, south to Bray. Scenic, cheap, essential for day trips.
Luas Tram
Red + Green lines cross the city. Red line good for Kilmainham and Smithfield area.
Language
English (and Irish/Gaeilge on signs). Learn: "Sláinte!" (cheers!) for maximum pub credibility.
