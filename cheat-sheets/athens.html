Athens

The birthplace of democracy, philosophy, and the Olympics. Athens sits under the Acropolis like a living museum — ancient ruins surrounded by a modern, chaotic, delicious city that surprises every visitor.

🍽 What to Eat
Souvlaki
Grilled meat in pita with tzatziki and tomato. The original Greek street food.
Moussaka
Layered eggplant, minced lamb, and béchamel. The national comfort dish.
Spanakopita
Flaky phyllo pastry stuffed with spinach and feta. Perfect street snack.
Horiatiki
Real Greek salad — no lettuce. Tomato, cucumber, olives, feta block on top.
Fresh Seafood
Grilled octopus, sea bass, and calamari. Head to Piraeus for the best.
Loukoumades
Honey-drizzled Greek donuts. Get them warm from street vendors in Monastiraki.
Baklava
Layers of phyllo with honey and pistachios. Try it fresh from a bakery.
Ouzo + Mezedes
Anise spirit served with small plates. Always enjoyed with food, never alone.
💡 Local Tips
◆ Book Acropolis and Acropolis Museum tickets online well in advance — they sell out completely in peak season.
◆ Visit the Acropolis at 8am when it opens — heat and crowds both increase dramatically after 10am.
◆ The Athens transport day pass covers metro, bus, and tram. Buy at the airport machine on arrival.
◆ Tipping 5–10% in restaurants is appreciated but not mandatory. Round up for good service.
◆ Diporto is cash only and has no sign — look for a basement entrance near Sofokleous Street market.
◆ Psiri and Monastiraki are the best neighborhoods for nightlife and late-night eating.
◆ Avoid July–August if heat is a concern. September–October is the sweet spot: warm, less crowded.
◆ Athens runs on late schedules — dinner starts at 9pm. Don't show up at a restaurant at 7pm and wonder why it's empty.
🏨 Where to Stay
Hotel Grande Bretagne ★★★★★
Syntagma Square
The legendary Athens grand dame. Rooftop pool and restaurant with Acropolis views. The most iconic address in the city.
Electra Metropolis ★★★★
Syntagma / Plaka
Rooftop infinity pool with breathtaking Acropolis views. Modern design, great location, consistently excellent service.
Hotel Herodion ★★★★
Near Acropolis
Walk to the Acropolis in 5 minutes. Rooftop restaurant with direct views of the Parthenon. Excellent value for location.
Budget Tip
Monastiraki or Psiri
Stay in Monastiraki or Psiri for the best bang for your buck. Central, walkable to everything, great street food at the door.
🗺 Getting Around
Airport → Center
Metro Line 3 direct to Syntagma (40 min, €9). Taxi ~€40, 30–60 min.
Metro
3 lines cover key sights. Day pass €4.50. Validate before boarding.
Walking
Central Athens is very walkable. Plaka → Monastiraki → Psiri all on foot.
Taxis / Rideshare
Bolt and Free Now apps work well. Cheaper than metered taxis.
Day Trips
Aegina: Piraeus ferry. Cape Sounion: KTEL bus from Areos Park (€6.50).
Language
Greek. English widely spoken in tourist areas. Learn: "Efharisto" (thank you).
